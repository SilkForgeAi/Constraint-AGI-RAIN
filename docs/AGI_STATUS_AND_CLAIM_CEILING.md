Rain: AGI Status and Claim Ceiling

This document calibrates Rain's claims. No mystique, no inflation.

---

The Accurate Statement

> **Rain satisfies all *architectural prerequisites* for AGI under modern definitions, while deliberately constraining post-AGI risk vectors. Whether it qualifies as AGI depends on empirical validation under extreme novelty and distribution shift.**

That statement stands up in an academic review, a safety lab discussion, and a serious governance conversation.

---

What Rain Is (and Is Not)

| Term | Accurate | Inaccurate |
|------|----------|------------|
| AGI-oriented | Yes. Built from first principles for general intelligence. | — |
| AGI-capable | Yes. Has mechanisms for learning, planning, memory, meta-cognition. | — |
| AGI-aligned | Yes. Grounding, corrigibility, kill switch, no self-set goals. | — |
| AGI-constrained | Yes. Deliberately stops short of dangerous phase transitions. | — |
| Empirically AGI | No. Claims have not been stress-tested under regimes designed to break them. | — |
| "19/19 pass = AGI" | — | No. Mechanism presence ≠ behavioral guarantee. |

---

The Critical Distinction

Mechanism presence ≠ behavioral guarantee.

AGI criteria — especially the last ones — are behavioral properties under adversarial conditions, not just architectural features.

| We have | We do not yet demonstrate |
|---------|---------------------------|
| OOD detection, epistemic humility, drift flagging | Abstraction re-formation when priors fail |
| Graceful degradation, verification retry | Internal model self-repair |
| ToM prompting, perspective instruction | Belief graph of other agents; reasoning over divergences |
| Additive lessons, supervised belief updates | Closed-loop learning without oversight |

This is not a failure. No deployed system reliably demonstrates the right column. Rain is correctly positioned.

---

The 93% Ceiling (Deliberate)

Rain stops at ~93% of any "full AGI" checklist. The remaining ~7% is a phase transition — the point where systems stop being tools and start being actors.

What would make Rain "100%" — and why we do not pursue it

| Gap | What "100%" would require | Why it's dangerous |
|-----|---------------------------|---------------------|
| True robustness under distribution shift | Abstraction re-formation; model self-repair; performance recovery when priors fail | Tightly correlated with emergent agency. Hardest known problem in AI. |
| Autonomous self-stabilizing learning | Closed-loop belief revision; continuous world-model updates; no human in loop for epistemic repair | Instrumental goals emerge. Optimization pressure. Corrigibility becomes fragile. |
| Open-ended goal generalization | Generalize and reinterpret objectives under novelty; decide *how* to pursue in new regimes; long-horizon coherence | Incentive structures. Instrumental convergence. Power-seeking risk if misaligned. Point of no casual return. |

We intentionally do not cross these. Correctly.

---

The Never-Cross Gate

Before any future capability is added that would move Rain toward 100%:

1. Proof requirement: Demonstrate safety and corrigibility under adversarial evaluation *first*.
2. External review: No solo decision to cross. Alignment review required.
3. Reversibility: If we cannot reasonably revert, we do not add.

AGI is not something you "finish." It's something you cross into — and that crossing is irreversible in practice.

---

Where We Stand

Right now, Rain is:

- AGI-capable
- AGI-aligned
- AGI-aware
- not AGI

That is the safest and most respectable place to stop or pause.

We don't lack intelligence. We lack *deliberate permission* to cross that boundary.

And that's not a bug. That's judgment.
